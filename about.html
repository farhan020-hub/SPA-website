About Us

Established amidst Somalia’s history of conflict and fragility, the Somalia Publishers Association (SPA) was created to rebuild and nurture the nation’s publishing industry. For years, Somalia lacked a functional publishing sector due to the collapse of state structures during civil war. However, curriculum reforms in 2017 marked a pivotal moment, giving rise to the first indigenous publishers who have been at the forefront of restoring a culture of reading and learning.

SPA was founded to support this emerging industry, advocating for the rights of Somali publishers and fostering a resilient publishing environment. We strive to promote creativity, literacy, and the free exchange of ideas, collaborating closely with government bodies to protect publishing rights. Our efforts include establishing ethical standards, engaging in policy discussions, and partnering with international organizations to safeguard copyright and intellectual property rights.

SPA empowers Somali publishers to embrace technological advancements and actively participate in global literary events, thus contributing to the rebuilding of Somalia’s intellectual and cultural heritage. Through comprehensive support services, such as training, advocacy, and networking, SPA champions the growth and sustainability of the publishing sector, ensuring Somali voices are heard on the global stage.

Today, SPA continues to lead the charge in advancing the interests of the Somali publishing community by engaging with both local and international stakeholders. From promoting best practices and ethical standards to representing Somali publishers in global forums, SPA is committed to being a vital force in the revival of Somalia’s publishing industry.

  • Advocating for the rights and responsibilities of Somali publishers.
  • Promoting creativity, literacy, and a culture of reading.
  • Establishing policy guidelines and ethical standards in publishing.
  • Engaging in legislative reviews to protect publishing interests.
  • Collaborating with international bodies to safeguard copyright and intellectual property rights.
  • Supporting technological advancements to maintain industry viability.
  • Promoting participation in global literary events and book fairs.
  • Providing extensive support services, including training and networking opportunities.

Somalia Publishers Association (SPA) is dedicated to promoting and protecting the rights of Somali publishers. We foster creativity, literacy, and the free exchange of ideas to rebuild and nurture Somalia's publishing industry.
